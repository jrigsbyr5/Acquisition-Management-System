About Land & Acquisition Management — Philadelphia Development Intelligence

Overview

Alterra is a development intelligence platform built for Philadelphia land acquisition and financialfeasibility analysis. It aggregates parcel-level zoning, cost modeling, and market data into a single spatial interface — designed for developers, planners, and investors who want to leverage the power of data to forcast development scenarios and make land/building aquisition decisions.

Cost Projection

This interactive financial modeling and development forcasting tool delivers acquisition recommendations for every parcel in the city. By clicking any parcel lot dimensions, zoning capacity, FAR headroom, a full development cost estimate — hard cost, soft cost, contingency, and total development basis are calculated and available for use. The Simulate Scenario tool can be used to run custom assumptions, making any portion of the financial model directly editable, enabling the survery of endless cost estimate scenarios per parcel.

Market Activity

A heatmap of development permitting and construction activity across Philadelphia by time period. Visualize where the market has moved across almost two decades. revealing inflection points where and when it made sense to buy property. This insight can guide future aquisition strategies as it reveals where these inflections points are headed.

Pricing

Tract-level price per square foot analysis for land sales, segmented by transaction type and time period. Identify underpriced sectors and track how land values have shifted across the city over almosttwo decades.

Data

Built on Philadelphia OPA parcel data, L&I permits, and market transaction records. Zoning and development capacity derived from Philadelphia’s current zoning code.

About This View
Each parcel is colored by its aggregated median of means for price per square foot of land sales. Darker reds indicate higher land values. Use the time period and transaction type filters above to explore how Philadelphia land prices have shifted across different markets and time periods.
Philadelphia Vacant Land
Philadelphia has one of the largest concentrations of vacant land of any major U.S. city — an estimated 40,000+ parcels. These lots reflect decades of population decline, fleeting investment, and industrial abandonment.
Transaction Types
Vacant Land — sales of parcels with no structures, representing raw land value. Market Sales — arm's-length transactions reflecting actual buyer/seller market pricing. Both — combined view showing all qualifying transactions for the period.

About This View
Each census tract is colored by the median price per square foot of land sales aggregated across all parcels in that tract. Darker reds indicate higher land values. Use the time period and transaction type filters to compare how tract-level land prices have shifted across Philadelphia over time.
Why Census Tracts
Individual parcels rarely have sales in every time period, making direct comparison unreliable. Aggregating to the census tract level pools enough transactions per unit to produce stable, comparable price signals across all four periods — revealing neighborhood-level appreciation trends.
Transaction Types
Vacant Land — tracts aggregated from parcels with no structures, representing raw land value. Market Sales — arm's-length transactions reflecting actual buyer/seller market pricing. Both — combined view showing all qualifying transactions for the period.
